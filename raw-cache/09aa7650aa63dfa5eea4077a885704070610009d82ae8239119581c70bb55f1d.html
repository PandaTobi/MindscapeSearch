Welcome to the August 2023 Ask Me Anything episode of Mindscape! These monthly excursions are funded by Patreon supporters (who are also the ones asking the questions). We take questions asked by Patreons, whittle them down to a more manageable number -- based primarily on whether I have anything interesting to say about them, not whether the questions themselves are good -- and sometimes group them together if they are about a similar topic. Enjoy!

    Regarding Huge Everett’s many-worlds (MWI), which always seems to come up in these Ask Me Anything episodes of Mindscape, MWI’s initial reception was overwhelming negative, in the sense that it was ignored by most of the scientific community. John Archibold Wheeler, Everett’s Princeton PhD advisor who made considerable efforts to formulate the theory in a way that would be palatable to Niels Bohr, visited Copenhagen in 1956 to discuss it with him, and convinced Everett to visit as well, which happened in 1959. Nevertheless, Bohr and his collaborators completely rejected the theory. Everett had already left academia in 1956, never to return, and after his death, Wheeler disavowed the theory.

For those who believe in the literal interpretation of MWI, that was the exact outcome in some ‘universe’.
